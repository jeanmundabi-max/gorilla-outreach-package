We already use Aurora for forecasting. Why do we need Gorilla on top?
Aurora tells you what the wholesale electricity cost will be. It does not touch your billing data, settlement actuals, or customer-level margin position. The gap between what Aurora models and what you actually earn in settlement is exactly where margin disappears. Gorilla closes that gap by unifying wholesale cost, settlement data, and billing in a single view. Most Gorilla customers continue using Aurora — different tools, different questions.
Our ETRM system handles our hedging. What does Gorilla add?
Your ETRM handles trade execution and hedge position management. It does not tell you how effective those hedges are against your actual customer margin position — because it cannot see your retail billing and settlement data. Gorilla sits above the ETRM, billing, and MDM systems and shows the unified picture. Hedge book effectiveness against actual retail margin, live, not monthly.
We built an internal pricing model. It's working fine for now.
The question is whether it handles MHHS data volumes. Pre-MHHS settlement produces one data point per meter per day. MHHS produces 48.
