Go-to-Market Plan

5 Pillars. 10-Day Launch. 12-Week Roadmap.

The full commercial infrastructure for reaching 40 mid-market UK and European energy retailers in active MHHS buying windows. Built on signal-based targeting, not broad awareness advertising.

5 Pillars
The infrastructure MDB installs for Gorilla's mid-market energy retailer acquisition
01
Signal Database
30 named UK and European energy retailers segmented by buying signal: MHHS migration status, pricing analyst job postings, Ofgem compliance data, Companies House director changes. Updated weekly. Tier 1: 15 companies with 2 or more confirmed signals.
02
3-Signal Outreach Model
Signal-triggered outreach within 72 hours of a buying signal firing. Three triggers: (1) Pricing role job posting — the loudest buying signal in the market. (2) MHHS milestone missed — empathetic outreach framing. (3) Director appointment — new CFO or Head of Commercial audit window.
03
Pre-Call Intelligence Briefs
Every company that responds to outreach gets a 1-page pre-call brief delivered to Gorilla's commercial team before the call. MHHS readiness score, estimated settlement leakage, primary objection likely, and specific case study most relevant to their customer count and product type.
04
Mid-Market Acquisition Page
A segment-specific landing page in the voice of the energy retailer CFO and Head of Pricing — built for the in-house build decision moment. Converts cold outreach targets who arrive curious into demo-booked prospects before Gorilla's commercial team makes a single call.
05
Margin Readiness Scorecard
8-question interactive tool that mid-market energy retailers complete before a demo. Scores their MHHS readiness and margin infrastructure gap. Gorilla's commercial team receives the completed scorecard before every call — the conversation starts at the specific gap, not at the beginning.
10-Day Launch Sequence
The first 10 days — reaching the Tier 1 list
Day 1
Database + Setup
Finalise Tier 1 list (15 companies). Confirm decision-maker contacts. Identify which companies Gorilla already has a relationship with — route those separately to Gorilla's commercial team, not to cold outreach.
Day 2
Email — Trigger Angle
email_1 (Trigger) sends to all 15 Tier 1 contacts. Subject: "40 UK energy retailers in a buying window." Delivery CTA: "I can send the list over." No package link in this email — Trigger angle earns the list reply.
Day 3
LinkedIn — Connection
LinkedIn connection requests send to all 15 Tier 1 decision-makers. Message references Utility Week Live coverage or Energy Margin Intelligence category launch — specific to each person's LinkedIn activity.
Day 4
LinkedIn — Text DM
Text hook DM after connection accepted. References a specific post or public statement from the decision-maker about MHHS migration pressure or in-house build frustration. Ends: "Sending a quick video shortly."
Day 5
Email — Friction Angle
email_2 (Friction) sends to all 15 contacts. Subject: "What LinkedIn ads miss for energy retailers." Package link included: "Built it already — [PACKAGE_URL]." Asks for a walkthrough of the Tier 1 list findings.
Day 6
LinkedIn — Video DM
80-second Loom video DM sends to all connected contacts. Enterprise Landing Page Explanation Framework — shows the mid-market challenger landing page and explains the conversion logic and objection handling built into it.
Day 7
Qualify Replies
First replies qualified. Any positive response gets a pre-call intelligence brief prepared. Company's MHHS status, estimated settlement leakage, likely objection, and most relevant case study all prepared before the call is booked.
Day 8
Tier 2 Launch
Tier 2 outreach begins. 15 European retailers and adjacent segment companies. Adjusted email framing for European regulatory context (e.g. Dutch ACM compliance milestones for Vandebron and ENECO instead of Ofgem Capital Target).
Day 9
Signal Monitoring
First weekly signal database update. Re-run job board monitoring across Energy Jobline, LinkedIn Jobs, and Indeed. Check Companies House for new director appointments at all 30 companies. Flag any new Tier 1 signals for immediate outreach.
Day 10
Pipeline Review
First pipeline visibility review with Gorilla's commercial team. Confirmed interested companies routed to Gorilla's demo calendar. New signals from weekly database update actioned. Adjust outreach framing based on reply data from first 5 days.
12-Week Roadmap
From database to pipeline to new mid-market contracts
Phase 1
Foundation and Launch
Weeks 1 to 4
Tier 1 signal database finalised and verified — 15 companies with 2 or more confirmed signals
email_1 (Trigger) and email_2 (Friction) sequences active across Tier 1 list
LinkedIn multi-step sequence running parallel to email — connection, text hook, video DM
Tier 2 European retailer outreach begins at week 2
First reply qualification underway — pre-call briefs prepared for positive responses
Target: 3 to 5 qualified conversations booked by end of week 4
Phase 2
Pipeline Build and Optimisation
Weeks 5 to 8
Weekly signal database updates — new job postings, director changes, Ofgem actions added within 72 hours
First wave of conversations completed — learnings applied to outreach framing and objection handling
Tier 1 non-responders re-engaged with a second signal-specific approach based on new database data
Gorilla case study relevance mapping — each active prospect matched to the Centrica or Synergy case study most relevant to their customer book size
Target: 1 to 2 pilots or demos progressed to commercial proposal stage by week 8
Phase 3
MHHS Window Close — Pre-October Sprint
Weeks 9 to 12
October 2026 MHHS 80% milestone urgency outreach — specific messaging to companies still in early MHHS readiness stages
Full database review — all 80 UK licensed suppliers checked against updated signal criteria
Scorecard completion data from the mid-market challenger page used to identify highest-readiness prospects for priority outreach
Gorilla's commercial team receives complete pre-call briefs for all active pipeline companies before any demo or discovery call
Target: 1 to 2 new mid-market contracts signed or in final negotiation by week 12
System handed to Gorilla as permanent owned infrastructure — not paused at end of engagement
