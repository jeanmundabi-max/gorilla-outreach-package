Revenue Leak Map
Where qualified mid-market suppliers currently exit Gorilla's funnel
Leak 1 — Invisible buying window
A mid-market supplier posts for a Senior Pricing Analyst on Energy Jobline. Gorilla's LinkedIn ads reach energy professionals broadly. They do not flag that posting to Gorilla's commercial team. The supplier hires the analyst. Two months later they start building internally. The buying window closes.
MDB fix: Signal database + 72-hour trigger outreach
Leak 2 — Enterprise case studies only
A Head of Pricing at a 180,000-customer mid-market supplier reads the Centrica case study. British Gas serves millions of customers — the data volumes, implementation complexity, and internal IT resources are completely different to their situation. She closes the tab. The connection is never made.
MDB fix: Mid-market segment landing page + pre-call brief
Leak 3 — In-house build decision made before Gorilla arrives
An energy retailer validates the MHHS data problem, decides to solve it with headcount, makes the hire, and the new data engineer starts building. Three weeks after the hire, Gorilla's outreach lands. The answer is always "we're handling it internally." The in-house build becomes entrenched. Gorilla is now competing with sunk cost, not a blank decision.
MDB fix: Job posting trigger outreach within 72 hours
Leak 4 — Demo without pre-qualification
An energy retailer books a demo. Gorilla's commercial team takes the call. It becomes clear in the first 10 minutes that the company has 15,000 customers, is pre-Series A, and does not have the integration infrastructure to deploy Gorilla for another 12 months. 45 minutes spent. No conversion. No structured path for when they are ready.
MDB fix: Scorecard qualification + staged pipeline routing
Closed — Enterprise Tier Acquisition (existing model)
Gorilla's existing customer acquisition model — founder-led relationships, conference presence, partner referrals — works extremely well for the enterprise tier. British Gas, Shell, EDF, SEFE, Drax, ScottishPower: all acquired through this channel. This is not a leak. This is a ceiling.
Status: Working — model does not scale to mid-market without MDB infrastructure
Closed — Thought Leadership and Category Creation
Gorilla's "Energy Margin Intelligence" category launch in November 2025, Reuters Awards nomination, and Utility Week Live presence create strong brand awareness in the market. Energy retailers know the name. The gap is converting that awareness into active pipeline conversations before buying windows close.
Status: Working — needs signal-based activation layer on top
Growth Feedback Loops
Three compounding loops that make Gorilla's mid-market acquisition stronger over time
01
Core Acquisition Loop — Signal to Contract
→MHHS migration signal fires (job posting, Ofgem action, director change)
→Trigger outreach within 72 hours — email or LinkedIn DM referencing the specific signal
→Prospect completes margin readiness scorecard — identifies own gap score
→Demo booked — Gorilla's team receives pre-call brief before the call
→Proposal issued — mid-market ACV £150,000 to £300,000
→Contract signed — new case study available for next target segment
Compounding effect Every new mid-market contract produces a case study comparable to the next prospect. The Centrica enterprise case study converts enterprise buyers. A Yü Energy mid-market case study converts mid-market buyers. The database grows stronger with each contract.
02
Credibility Loop — Case Study Compounding
→New mid-market customer signed and implementing Gorilla
→Customer achieves measurable result (settlement leakage reduced, MHHS readiness score improved, pricing time cut)
→Result quantified and added to case study library with comparable supplier profile
→Next prospect in same segment sees a supplier with the same customer count and product mix
→Self-identification happens — "that is us" — conversion friction drops
Compounding effect The third mid-market case study closes deals faster than the first because the evidence stack is growing. The conversion timeline shortens with each case study added at the relevant segment level.
03
Test and Scale Loop — Signal Quality Improvement
→Outreach sequence runs across Tier 1 list
→Reply data shows which signal type (job posting vs director change vs Ofgem action) converts at highest rate
→Database updated — highest-converting signal types weighted more heavily in Tier 1 scoring
→Outreach framing adjusted to match the signal type with the highest open and reply rate
→Next cycle produces higher reply rate and faster pipeline velocity
Compounding effect Gorilla owns the database and all signal data when the MDB engagement concludes. The system gets more accurate with every cycle because real-world response data improves the signal scoring model. It is not a campaign — it is infrastructure that learns.
